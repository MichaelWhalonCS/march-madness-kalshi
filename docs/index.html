▶ How Survivor Pools Work & How to Read This Table

Survivor Pool Rules

  • Pick one team per round that you think will win their game. If they win, you advance. If they lose, you're eliminated.
  • You can only use each team once for the entire tournament. Once you pick Duke in Round 1, you can never pick Duke again.
  • The goal is to survive all 6 rounds (R64 through the Championship). Last person standing wins the pool.

Data Sources

  • Kalshi Tournament Futures — Per-team, per-round advancement markets on a CFTC-regulated prediction exchange. For example, "Will Duke qualify for the Sweet Sixteen?" Real money is traded on every outcome, so prices aggregate the collective wisdom of thousands of traders. The percentage columns (Make R32, Make S16, etc.) are derived from these futures prices.
  • Kalshi Per-Game Markets — Binary win/loss markets for each individual game. The Kalshi column shows the market-implied probability for each team winning their current-round game, calculated as the midpoint of the best bid and ask prices. Each team's Kalshi cell links directly to the specific market for that game.

How the Numbers Are Calculated

  • Cumulative probabilities (the round columns) are derived from Kalshi tournament futures markets. "Make S16 = 65%" means the market gives that team a 65% chance of reaching the Sweet 16.
  • Conditional (game-win) probabilities — shown when you hover a cell — are derived by dividing consecutive rounds: P(win R32 game) = P(make S16) / P(make R32). This gives the probability of winning that specific game, given the team made it there.
  • Kalshi probability (per-game column) = midpoint of the market bid/ask spread for the individual game market. For example, if a team's YES contract is bid $0.72 / ask $0.74, the implied probability is 73%.
  • Win & Out = P(make next round) − P(make round after that). This is the probability a team wins now but loses its next game — a "one-and-done" indicator. High Win & Out teams are best used in the current round rather than saved for later.
  • Best Pick = the latest round where a team's conditional game-win probability is ≥ 70%. This tells you the optimal round to "use" a team. Don't waste a 1-seed on R64 if they're still 80% to win in R32 — save them for when you need them.

Futures vs. Per-Game Markets

  • Futures (the round columns) are forward-looking — they price in the strength of opponents the team is likely to face in later rounds. A 1-seed might be 97% to win R64 but only 55% to make the Elite 8 because of a tough bracket path.
  • Per-game (the Kalshi column) reflects only the current single game. It often matches the R64 futures closely but can diverge when late-breaking news (injuries, lineup changes) hits the per-game market faster.
  • When futures and per-game odds disagree significantly, it's worth investigating — one market may have priced in information the other hasn't yet.

Strategy Tips

  • Use mid-seeds (5–8) in early rounds where they're still heavy favorites. Save 1–2 seeds for later rounds when games get tighter.
  • Sort by "Best Pick" to see which teams give you the most value in each round.
  • If a team's conditional win % drops below 60% in a round, that's a risky pick for survivor.
  • Check the Win & Out column — teams with high Win & Out are "use it or lose it" picks.
