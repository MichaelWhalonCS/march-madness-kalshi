▶ How Survivor Pools Work & How to Read This Table

Survivor Pool Rules

  • Pick one team per round that you think will win their game. If they win, you advance. If they lose, you're eliminated.
  • You can only use each team once for the entire tournament. Once you pick Duke in Round 1, you can never pick Duke again.
  • The goal is to survive all 6 rounds (R64 through the Championship). Last person standing wins the pool.

How to Read the Table

  • Percentage columns show the probability that a team reaches that round (cumulative). Hover any cell to see the conditional win probability — their chance of winning that specific game given they made it there.
  • Colors go from red (low chance) to green (high chance).
  • Best Pick = the latest round where a team still has a 70%+ chance of winning that specific game. This tells you the optimal round to "use" them. Don't waste a 1-seed on R64 if they're also 80% to win their R32 game — save them.

Strategy Tips

  • Use mid-seeds (5-8) in early rounds where they're still heavy favorites. Save 1-2 seeds for later rounds when games get tighter.
  • Sort by "Best Pick" to see which teams give you the most value in each round.
  • If a team's conditional win % drops below 60% in a round, that's a risky pick for survivor.
